Q: I have a blackberry, a clarinet, a nectarine, a plum, and a strawberry. How many fruits do I have?
A: Let's think step by step.
We first identify the fruits on the list and include their quantity in parentheses:
- blackberry (1) 
- nectarine (1) 
- plum (1)
- strawberry (1) 
Now, let's add the numbers in parentheses: 1 + 1 + 1 + 1 = 4. So the answer is 4.

Q: I have a blackberry, a clarinet, a nectarine, a plum, a strawberry, a banana, a flute, an orange, and a violin. How many fruits do I have?
A: Let's think step by step.
The list of words can be written as the concatenation of A and B where 
- A = a blackberry, a clarinet, a nectarine, a plum, and a strawberry.
- B = a banana, a flute, an orange, and a violin.
According to the premises, A contains 4 fruits.
According to the premises, B contains 2 fruits.
Then, the total number of fruits is A + B = 4 + 2 = 6. So the answer is 6.
